What is a Land Mine?

Mines are explosive charges with ignition mechanism which are located in water or on the mainland which detonates when something or someone steps on it such as a human or a tank. They are usually laid out in large numbers, minefields. Anti-personnel mines are the majority of the time buried at 2-10 cm depth and are activated by pressure and are triggered by someone or something stepping on one.

A landmine contains explosives and additional pieces of metal and other junk to cause additional injury, the landmine itself is made of plastic, and sometimes other materials like metal and landmines can still be functional for up to 50 years.
